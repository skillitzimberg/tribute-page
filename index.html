Carl Sagan

  • Carl Edward Sagan was born in Brooklyn, NY on November 9, 1934.
  • He was admitted to the University of Chicago at the age of 16.
  • Due to his wide-ranging interests, Sagan was denied tenure at Harvard University.
  • His work as an advisor to NASA saw him coach the first astronauts, contribute to the robotic exploration of space, and compile the information on the gold disc launched with Pioneer 10.
  • Sagan was a staunch opponent of nuclear arms and took every opportunity to warn of the threat it poses.
